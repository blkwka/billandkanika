Project Scope

Our project focuses on developing an cutting edge mobile application game as a way of solving the widespread difficulties related to type 1 diabetes in our local community. More than just providing entertainment, our main goal is to spread important awareness about the daily challenges faced by people with diabetes. Our goal in creating this app is to create a unique and powerful resource that will not only amuse our intended audience but also act as a guide for increasing awareness as well as comprehension among members of our community.

insuLIN

Our app, InsuLIN, is intended to serve as a educational tool that provides information on the eating habits decisions, physical activity habits, and difference that exists between carbohydrates and protein consumption and insulin dosage in managing diabetes. Through their interaction with our app, we hope that users will not only collect a deep understanding of how to deal with the difficulties presented by diabetes but also encourage independence and well-informed decision making in our community.

Engagement

The primary goal of the app's strategic design is to engage a younger audience, specifically those under the age of 18. We strive to accommodate the teaching styles of our children because we recognize the difficulties that come with lengthy papers or complex passages. A researcher named Louisa Rosenheck at MIT’s Education Arcade Lab stated “What’s really motivating about a good learning game is the learning,”. Our strategy makes sure that crucial data about type 1 diabetes is not only available to but also enjoyable for our younger users by utilizing the power of game development. We recognize that using a game to teach medical knowledge creates a more interactive and exciting learning environment, which helps kids learn and remember critical information about managing their diabetes. This customized strategy improves the effectiveness of our community awareness campaign while simultaneously meeting the specific needs of our target market.
